Common Elevator Use

The most common use of an elevator is to move up and down from floor to floor. Another rarer use of an elevator is to move material and other goods inside mines and manufacturing plants.

Common Sequence of Actions

The common sequence of actions in using an elevator is

  1. The user presses the up or down arrow and waits for the door to open.
  2. Once the door opens, the user will press the number corresponding to the floor level they are going to.
  3. Once the elevator is on the correct floor level, the door will open, and the user will walk out.

How to make elevator work

The elevator panel's design is similar to how it will work. As the buttons are aligned vertically, starting from 1 on the bottom, the user will quickly understand how to use the panel. Furthermore, the alignment of the buttons and design of the panel reassures the users they are using it properly.

Elevator feedback

The elevator provides feedback couple of ways, such as with the red ring that lights up when you press a button. The elevator also makes loud beeps as it passes a floor.

Common Mistakes Made with Current Design

Some common mistakes you could make with this design would be accidentally pressing another button. Some users made the mistake of thinking floors 2,3 and 4 required keys to access that level each time.
